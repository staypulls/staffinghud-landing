THE PROBLEM
The Tools That Exist Are
Built for Someone Else.
Enterprise platforms like Bullhorn were designed for 50-person agencies with a dedicated IT team and a six-month implementation budget. Everyone else is duct-taping spreadsheets together and calling it a system.
PROBLEM · 01
No Placement Visibility
Every recruiter has their own tracker. Managers have no centralized view of what's active, what's overdue, or where placements are stalling. Status lives in someone's inbox or head — until it doesn't.
PROBLEM · 02
Job Orders Lost in Email
Client job orders come in through email, text, and phone. No queue, no priority, no assignment tracking. Recruiters find out what fell through when the client calls to ask why nobody followed up.
PROBLEM · 03
Compliance is a Manual Liability
I-9 deadlines, background check windows, drug screen timelines — tracked manually if at all. One missed step creates real legal exposure. There's no system enforcing the rules before they get broken.
PROBLEM · 04
Per-Seat Pricing Punishes Growth
Every recruiter you hire increases your software bill. Bullhorn charges $120–$200/user/month. A 10-person team costs $14,400–$24,000 per year — before add-ons. Growth should compound revenue, not overhead.
